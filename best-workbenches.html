Choosing the Right Workbench

Work Surface Material

Solid hardwood is the classic choice — it's durable, repairable (just sand it), and can hold a vise. Bamboo is harder and more moisture-resistant. Stainless steel is easy to clean but cold and noisy. MDF is cheap but swells with moisture. For a garage, hardwood or bamboo are the best choices.

Fixed vs. Adjustable Height

Adjustable height is worth the premium. Standard bench height (34-36") works for most standing tasks, but being able to lower it for sitting work or raise it for detail work is a luxury you'll appreciate. If you're tall or short, adjustable is essential.

Fixed vs. Folding vs. Rolling

Fixed benches are the sturdiest and best for heavy work. Rolling benches add flexibility but sacrifice some stability. Folding benches save space but can't be moved and have no built-in storage. Choose based on your garage size and how you work.

Weight Capacity

For general garage work, 500 pounds is adequate. For automotive work, woodworking, or mounting a vise, aim for 1,000+ pounds. The 3,000-pound benches are overkill for most people, but they'll never flex or wobble under any load.

And for maximum work surface, the Gladiator 6-foot is the premium choice.
